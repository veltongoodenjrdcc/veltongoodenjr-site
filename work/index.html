My Work

The portfolio is the overlap.

My background is not one straight corridor. It is a set of connected rooms: web builds, content, voice, communication, digital strategy, and the practical systems that make the visible work hold up.

How the range formed

The eras are different. The instincts keep repeating.

I have spent time around production, platforms, public communication, service businesses, and digital cleanup. Each lane sharpened a different part of the same muscle: seeing what people need to understand, then shaping the thing so they can actually move.

1

Creative production

Camera, content, voice, storytelling, editing, presentation, and the discipline of making an idea land in the real world.

2

Digital front doors

Websites, landing pages, page structure, trust signals, navigation, and the first impression people use to decide whether to keep going.

3

Communication systems

WhatsApp flows, saved replies, Google presence, follow-up logic, and the small operational pieces that stop interest from leaking away.

4

Integrated strategy

The current work: creative lens plus systems thinking, with enough digital brand strategist language to name the lane, and enough practical execution to make it useful.

Portfolio pockets

The areas of work that now converge

This is less about one big trophy project and more about the pattern across the work.

WebDigital presence

Websites and digital front doors

Structure, trust, clarity, and conversion paths

I think about a website as more than a page. It is the place where credibility, message, and next step have to stop arguing with each other.

  • Website projects for Jamaican businesses.
  • Page structure, copy flow, visual hierarchy, and practical calls to action.
SystemsLead flow

Lead flow and service-business cleanup

From online interest to actual next step

This is where the practical systems side shows up: less scattered WhatsApp, clearer enquiry paths, cleaner follow-up, and fewer warm leads fading out quietly.

  • WhatsApp Business, Google presence, saved replies, and simple lead tracking.
  • Offer shaping and message cleanup around the decision points that matter.
ContentStory

Content and communication work

Making people, ideas, and context easier to follow

Content taught me how quickly people tune out when the point is buried. The useful work is often deciding what matters, what to cut, and what order the story needs.

  • UWI Mona content and communication work.
  • Editing, framing, campaign support, and audience-aware communication.
VoiceDelivery

Voice, tone, and performance

Sounding credible, clear, and suited to the moment

Voice work sharpened my ear for pacing, emphasis, restraint, and tone. That translates back into copy, content, presentation, and brand communication.

  • Voice work connected to HBO Max, Cari-Med Group, and Montego Bay Convention Centre.
  • A stronger instinct for when language sounds natural, forced, warm, or overcooked.
BrandStrategy

Brand clarity and practical strategy

Positioning, message, and next-step clarity

The strategy part is not about making things sound inflated. It is about naming the real problem, shaping the message, and making the next move obvious.

  • Positioning, offer framing, page copy, and decision-path cleanup.
  • Useful strategy that can survive contact with a real business day.
CollaborationSupport

Creative and operational support

The connective tissue around the visible output

Some of the most valuable work is not glamorous. It is clarifying the brief, tightening the path, noticing the gap, and helping the thing get finished properly.

  • Campaign, content, web, and communication support across different contexts.
  • A working style that cares about taste, usefulness, and handover.
The through-line

Creative lens. Systems thinking. Practical delivery.

That is the convergence. I can care about the feel of the message and the shape of the process. I can look at the page, the reply, the story, the offer, and the handoff in one pass.

  • When something needs to look better, I ask what it needs to do better.
  • When something needs a system, I ask how people will actually use it.
  • When something needs a message, I ask what the audience has to understand before they can move.

If the overlap is what you need, start with the problem in front of you.

Tell me what feels unclear, leaky, or harder than it should be. I can usually help you find the practical next move.
